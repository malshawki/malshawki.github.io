About

I am a senior researcher with a passion for designing secure and reliable protocols that underpin today’s distributed systems. I work at the intersection of security, distributed systems, and cryptography where protocols are complex, adversaries are imaginative, and bugs have a remarkable sense of timing. My work spans a range of interconnected domains, including federated learning, network security, and applied cryptography, fields that sit at the core of safeguarding privacy, integrity, and trust in decentralized environments.

Over the years, I have led and contributed to numerous academic and industry-driven research initiatives, many of which focus on translating theoretical advances into practical, deployable solutions. Along the way, I’ve partnered with academic institutions, research labs, and companies to tackle security challenges that are anything but hypothetical. These collaborations have involved a diverse array of partners—from tech industry leaders to interdisciplinary research groups across institutions—bridging the gap between rigorous research and real-world impact.

Mentorship has been a cornerstone of my academic journey. I’ve had the privilege of supervising a large number of graduate students and early-career researchers, many of whom have gone on to establish successful paths in academia and industry. I continue to find inspiration in guiding new minds through the evolving challenges and frontiers of secure distributed computing.

In parallel with research, I have taught extensively at the undergraduate and graduate levels, offering courses and seminars that span cryptographic protocols, secure systems design, and advanced topics in computer networking. My approach balances foundational theory with practical insight, plus the occasional cautionary tale of what not to do in a production environment.

My current research focuses on designing resilient security architectures for collaborative machine learning environments, cryptographic tools for privacy-preserving data exchange, and adaptive protocol design for large-scale, distributed infrastructures. The work remains open to new ideas, collaborative efforts, and thought-provoking dialogue—particularly those that challenge assumptions and spark intellectual curiosity.

I am always interested in new ideas, collaborations, and discussions, whether you’re a fellow researcher, a prospective student, or someone navigating the intersection of cryptography and system security.
